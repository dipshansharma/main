Nepal to UK: The Reality Check

The UK weather is vastly different. It's not necessarily sub-zero freezing like the Himalayas, but it is constantly damp, windy, and grey. Here is how to pack smart and save your 30kg baggage allowance.

MUST Bring from Nepal

  • Cultural Wear: Kurta, Daura Suruwal, or Sarees. You will want these for Uni cultural nights and Dashain/Tihar events. They are very expensive in the UK.
  • Specific Spices: Good quality Timur, Momos masala, or specific local teas. General spices (turmeric, cumin) are cheap here, but niche Nepali spices are hard to find.
  • Good Thermals: Buy 2-3 pairs of high-quality thermal innerwear in Nepal. They are a lifesaver against the damp UK cold.
  • Universal Adapters: Bring at least two Type-G (UK 3-pin) travel adapters.
  • Pressure Cooker: If you love traditional cooking, a small Nepali/Indian style pressure cooker is great, though you can buy them in the UK if needed.

Do NOT Bring (Buy in UK)

  • Heavy Blankets & Pillows: Takes up half your suitcase. Buy a 10.5 tog duvet at Primark, IKEA, or Asda for £15 when you arrive.
  • Pots, Pans & Rice Cookers: Go to B&M, Poundland, or Argos. You can get cheap student kitchen sets for under £20.
  • Massive Winter Coats: UK cold is wet and windy. Heavy wool coats from Nepal often get ruined. Buy a waterproof, insulated jacket here from Vinted (second-hand app) for cheap.
  • Bulk Rice/Lentils: Asian supermarkets in the UK sell 10kg bags of Basmati and Dal for very cheap. Don't waste baggage weight.
  • Stationery: Notebooks and pens are incredibly cheap at Poundland.

Work Rules & Hustles

The 20-Hour Rule & Self-Employment Ban

You are strictly limited to 20 hours per week during active term time. You can work full-time only during official holidays.

CRITICAL: International students cannot be self-employed. No driving for Uber, no freelance contracting, and Deliveroo/UberEats is usually a no-go unless you find an official PAYE contract (which is rare).
